Kurt Godel


The greatest logician of all time



Here are some of the most important events in Kurt Godel's life...


  • Born April 28, 1906 in Brunn, Austria-Hungary
  • Attends the Evangelische Volksschule from 1912-1916.
  • Attends the Deutsches Staats-Realgymnasium from 1916-1924. Graduates with highest honors
  • Enrolls in the University of Vienna in 1924. Receives his PhD. in Mathematics 5 years later at the age of 23
  • Proves the completeness of First-Order Predicate Logic in his doctoral dissertation.
  • Proves the monumentally influential Incompleteness Theorem at the age of 25, forever changing the face of Logic
  • Proves that Intuitionistic not finitely-valued in 1932
  • Proves that Intuitionistic Logic is intepretable in Sytem S4 of Modal Logic in 1933
  • Proves the Speed-Up Theorem in 1936
  • Proves in 1940 that both the Axiom of Choice and the Continuum Hypothesis are consistent with the Axioms of Set Theory
  • After 1940, his interests begin to shift to more purely Philosophical questions
  • Spends the last decades of his life arguing for Metaphysical Realism and attempting to revive Rationalism (a way of doing Philosophy that had gone of of favor in Godel's time and is still out of favor today)
  • Formulates a version of the Ontological Proof for God's existence in the early 1970's, but only privately circulates the manuscript among his closest friends.
  • Dies from starvation on January 14, 1978

"Kurt Gödel's achievement in modern logic is singular and monumental – indeed it is more than a monument, it is a landmark which will remain visible far in space and time. ... The subject of logic has certainly completely changed its nature and possibilities with Gödel's achievement."

- Jon Von Neumann
